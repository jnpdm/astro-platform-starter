# Technical Account Manager (TAM) User Guide

## Overview

As a Technical Account Manager (TAM), you provide ongoing technical support and relationship management after partner launch. You work with partners during Gate 3 (Ready to Deliver) and throughout the Post-Launch phase to ensure operational success and identify growth opportunities.

## Your Responsibilities

- Support partners during Gate 3 launch validation
- Provide ongoing technical support post-launch
- Monitor partner operational health
- Identify and resolve technical issues
- Drive adoption and utilization
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Maintain long-term partner relationships
- Coordinate with PSM during transition

## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to the Partner Onboarding Hub URL
2. Click "Log In" in the top right corner
3. Use your Netlify Identity credentials
4. You'll be redirected to the dashboard

### Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard displays:

- **Your Partners**: Partners in Gate 3 and Post-Launch phases
- **Filtered View**: Only partners where you're assigned as TAM
- **Health Status**: Operational health indicators for each partner
- **Recent Activity**: Support tickets, incidents, and partner interactions

## Working with Partners

### Initial Engagement (Gate 3)

1. **Handoff from PSM**
   - Review partner background
   - Understand operational setup
   - Review beta testing results
   - Discuss partner team dynamics

2. **Partner Introduction**
   - Meet partner technical and operations teams
   - Establish communication channels
   - Set expectations for ongoing support
   - Schedule regular check-ins

3. **Launch Support**
   - Be available during launch
   - Monitor initial operations
   - Respond quickly to issues
   - Provide reassurance and guidance

### Ongoing Support (Post-Launch)

1. **Regular Check-Ins**
   - Weekly calls during first month
   - Bi-weekly calls during months 2-3
   - Monthly calls thereafter
   - Ad-hoc support as needed

2. **Health Monitoring**
   - Review operational metrics
   - Monitor system performance
   - Track support tickets
   - Identify trends and issues

3. **Business Reviews**
   - Quarterly business reviews
   - Review performance metrics
   - Discuss challenges and opportunities
   - Plan improvements and expansions

## Best Practices

### Relationship Management

**Do**:
- Build trust through reliability
- Be proactive in communication
- Understand partner's business
- Celebrate successes
- Be honest about limitations
- Follow through on commitments

**Don't**:
- Only engage when there are problems
- Over-promise and under-deliver
- Ignore partner feedback
- Assume you know their needs
- Forget to say thank you
- Disappear after launch

### Technical Support

**Do**:
- Respond promptly to issues
- Provide clear explanations
- Document all interactions
- Follow up on resolutions
- Share knowledge proactively
- Escalate when appropriate

**Don't**:
- Let tickets go stale
- Provide incomplete solutions
- Skip root cause analysis
- Ignore recurring issues
- Work in isolation
- Forget to close the loop

### Business Reviews

**Do**:
- Prepare thoroughly
- Use data to tell the story
- Focus on business outcomes
- Identify opportunities
- Set clear action items
- Follow up on commitments

**Don't**:
- Wing it without preparation
- Focus only on problems
- Use jargon without explanation
- Skip the meeting
- Forget to document outcomes
- Fail to follow through

### Expansion Opportunities

**Do**:
- Understand partner's roadmap
- Identify natural expansion points
- Quantify potential value
- Coordinate with sales team
- Support expansion planning
- Celebrate growth

**Don't**:
- Push products they don't need
- Ignore their priorities
- Compete with sales team
- Promise what you can't deliver
- Forget the existing relationship
- Focus only on upsell

## Troubleshooting

### Partner Not Appearing in Dashboard

- Verify you're assigned as TAM owner
- Check partner's current gate (you see Gate 3 and Post-Launch)
- Confirm partner has launched
- Contact PAM to verify assignment

### Cannot Access Partner Information

- Verify your TAM role assignment
- Check partner's current phase
- Confirm you have appropriate permissions
- Contact support if issue persists

### Support Ticket Not Visible

- Check ticket system integration
- Verify ticket is assigned to partner
- Confirm ticket status
- Contact support team
