# Partner Development Manager (PDM) User Guide

## Overview

As a Partner Development Manager (PDM), you provide strategic technical support during pre-contract engagement and lead the onboarding process through Gate 1 (Ready to Sell). You work closely with PAMs to qualify partners and ensure successful GTM strategy development and training.

## Your Responsibilities

- Provide pre-contract technical consultation (10-15 hours/week)
- Validate partner qualification criteria
- Lead Gate 0 and Gate 1 activities
- Develop GTM strategies with partners
- Coordinate technical discovery
- Oversee partner training and enablement
- Manage 6-8 concurrent partners (capacity target)

## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to the Partner Onboarding Hub URL
2. Click "Log In" in the top right corner
3. Use your Netlify Identity credentials
4. You'll be redirected to the dashboard

### Dashboard Overview

Your dashboard displays:

- **Your Partners**: Partners in Pre-Contract through Gate 1 phases
- **Filtered View**: Only partners where you're assigned as PDM
- **Capacity Metrics**: Current partner load and hours allocated
- **Pending Actions**: Questionnaires awaiting your input

## Your Focus Areas

### Pre-Contract Phase

**Your Role**: Provide limited strategic technical support before contract signature

**Time Allocation**: 10-15 hours per week per partner

**Activities**:
- Validate executive sponsorship
- Assess commercial framework alignment
- Identify technical feasibility questions
- Evaluate strategic partner classification
- Determine if partner qualifies for PDM support

**Key Decision**: Should we allocate PDM resources to this partner?

### Gate 0: Onboarding Kickoff

**Your Role**: Validate readiness for white-glove onboarding

**Activities**:
- Verify contract execution
- Confirm partner team commitment
- Validate launch timing (within 12 months)
- Assess financial bar (CCV thresholds)
- Evaluate strategic value
- Check operational readiness

**Qualification Rules**:
- Tier 0 partners (CCV ≥$50M): Automatically qualify
- Other partners: Must meet at least 4 of 6 criteria
- Failure to meet criteria: Blocks progression

### Gate 1: Ready to Sell (Weeks 1-12)

**Your Role**: Lead partner through onboarding, GTM development, and training

**Phase 1A: Onboarding Kickoff & Planning (Weeks 1-3)**
- Conduct kickoff session
- Create detailed project plan
- Establish communication cadence
- Define success metrics

**Phase 1B: GTM Strategy & Technical Discovery (Weeks 3-6)**
- Develop GTM strategy
- Conduct technical discovery sessions
- Define technical architecture
- Identify integration requirements

**Phase 1C: Training & Enablement (Weeks 7-12)**
- Deliver product training
- Certify sales team
- Provide portal access
- Validate product knowledge

## Working with Questionnaires

### Pre-Contract PDM Engagement Questionnaire

**When to Complete**: With PAM, before contract signature

**Your Focus Sections**:

1. **Executive Sponsorship Confirmed**
   - Verify genuine executive commitment
   - Assess sponsor's influence and authority
   - Document sponsor engagement level

2. **Technical Feasibility / Partner Operational Questions**
   - Identify key technical questions
   - Assess partner's technical capabilities
   - Document operational concerns
   - Determine if questions are answerable pre-contract

3. **Strategic Partner Classification**
   - Validate CCV against Country LRP threshold (≥10%)
   - Assess strategic value beyond financials
   - Recommend tier classification

**Pass/Fail Evaluation**:
- Review each section's status
- Provide detailed notes for failures
- Work with PAM to address gaps
- Make go/no-go recommendation

### Gate 0: Onboarding Kickoff Questionnaire

**When to Complete**: After contract signature, before beginning onboarding

**Your Evaluation**:
- Assess all six readiness criteria
- Verify partner team composition
- Validate launch timeline feasibility
- Confirm operational readiness

**Decision Point**: Is this partner ready for white-glove onboarding?

### Gate 1: Ready to Sell Questionnaire

**When to Complete**: After completing Phases 1A, 1B, and 1C

**Your Validation**:
- Project plan approved and on track
- GTM strategy comprehensive and approved
- Technical architecture defined and documented
- Sales team certified and confident
- Product knowledge validated
- Portal access confirmed and tested

**Sign-Off**: Your signature indicates partner is ready to sell

## Capacity Management

### Target Capacity

- **Optimal**: 6-8 concurrent partners
- **Pre-Contract**: 10-15 hours/week per partner
- **Active Onboarding**: 15-20 hours/week per partner

### Monitoring Your Capacity

1. Navigate to Reports → PDM Analytics
2. View current partner load
3. Check hours allocated per partner
4. Review capacity utilization percentage

### Managing Overload

If you're approaching capacity:
1. Communicate with leadership
2. Prioritize Tier 0/1 partners
3. Consider delaying new pre-contract engagements
4. Escalate resource needs

## Best Practices

### Pre-Contract Engagement

**Do**:
- Focus on strategic qualification questions
- Set clear expectations about limited support
- Document all technical questions
- Provide honest assessment of fit

**Don't**:
- Over-invest time before contract signature
- Make commitments beyond pre-contract scope
- Skip qualification criteria
- Proceed without executive sponsorship

### Onboarding Leadership

**Do**:
- Establish clear communication cadence
- Set realistic timelines
- Document all decisions
- Celebrate milestone completions

**Don't**:
- Rush through discovery
- Skip training validation
- Assume partner knowledge
- Proceed with gaps in readiness

### GTM Strategy Development

**Do**:
- Understand partner's market and customers
- Align on value proposition
- Define clear success metrics
- Create actionable go-to-market plan

**Don't**:
- Use generic templates without customization
- Skip competitive analysis
- Ignore partner's existing sales motion
- Overlook channel conflicts

### Training Delivery

**Do**:
- Tailor training to partner's needs
- Validate understanding through assessments
- Provide hands-on practice
- Offer ongoing support resources

**Don't**:
- Use one-size-fits-all approach
- Skip certification validation
- Assume training retention
- Move forward without confidence

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario: Partner Doesn't Qualify for PDM Support

1. Review Pre-Contract questionnaire results
2. Identify specific gaps (e.g., no executive sponsorship)
3. Discuss with PAM
4. Provide clear feedback to partner
5. Document decision rationale

### Scenario: Partner Failing Gate 1 Criteria

1. Review specific failure reasons
2. Create remediation plan
3. Provide additional training or resources
4. Set timeline for re-assessment
5. Document progress

### Scenario: Partner Timeline Slipping

1. Identify root cause (partner bandwidth, technical issues, etc.)
2. Adjust project plan
3. Communicate with PAM and partner
4. Reset expectations
5. Update target dates

### Scenario: Capacity Constraints

1. Review current partner load
2. Prioritize by tier and strategic value
3. Communicate constraints to leadership
4. Delay lower-priority engagements
5. Request additional PDM resources if needed

## Troubleshooting

### Partner Not Appearing in Dashboard

- Verify you're assigned as PDM owner
- Check partner's current gate (you only see Pre-Contract through Gate 1)
- Refresh the page
- Contact PAM to verify assignment

### Cannot Complete Questionnaire

- Ensure you have PDM role assigned
- Verify all required fields are filled
- Check for validation errors
- Provide digital signature

### Gate Progression Blocked

- Review previous gate status
- Check for unmet criteria
- Consult with PAM
- Address specific failure reasons
