# Partner Success Manager (PSM) User Guide

## Overview

As a Partner Success Manager (PSM), you lead the operational readiness and launch validation phase during Gate 3 (Ready to Deliver). You work with partners to ensure they're operationally ready to deliver Kuiper services to customers, conducting beta testing and validating operational metrics.

## Your Responsibilities

- Lead Gate 3: Ready to Deliver activities (Weeks 18-20)
- Oversee operational readiness validation
- Coordinate beta testing with real customers
- Validate operational metrics and KPIs
- Ensure support transition is complete
- Verify launch readiness
- Coordinate with TPM (handoff) and TAM (transition)

## Getting Started

### Logging In

1. Navigate to the Partner Onboarding Hub URL
2. Click "Log In" in the top right corner
3. Use your Netlify Identity credentials
4. You'll be redirected to the dashboard

Your dashboard displays:

- **Your Partners**: Partners in Gate 3 (Ready to Deliver) phase
- **Filtered View**: Only partners where you're assigned as PSM
- **Launch Status**: Beta testing and validation progress
- **Pending Actions**: Operational validations awaiting completion

## Your Focus Area

### Gate 3: Ready to Deliver (Weeks 18-20)

**Your Role**: Validate operational readiness and launch preparation

**Phase 3A: Operational Readiness (Weeks 18-19)**

Activities:
- Conduct operational readiness review
- Validate support processes and escalation paths
- Verify customer communication templates
- Confirm billing and invoicing setup
- Review service level agreements (SLAs)
- Validate incident management procedures
- Ensure capacity planning is complete

**Phase 3B: Launch Validation (Week 20)**

Activities:
- Coordinate beta testing with real customers
- Monitor beta customer experience
- Validate operational metrics (order success rate, fulfillment time, etc.)
- Review customer feedback
- Verify support ticket handling
- Confirm launch readiness
- Plan go-live activities

## Operational Readiness Process

### Phase 1: Support Process Validation

1. **Review Support Structure**
   - Verify support team staffing
   - Confirm support hours and coverage
   - Review support tools and systems
   - Validate knowledge base

2. **Test Escalation Paths**
   - Document escalation procedures
   - Test escalation workflows
   - Verify contact information
   - Confirm response time SLAs

3. **Validate Communication Templates**
   - Review customer onboarding emails
   - Check service notification templates
   - Verify incident communication
   - Test customer portal messaging

### Phase 2: Operational Systems Validation

1. **Billing and Invoicing**
   - Verify billing system integration
   - Test invoice generation
   - Confirm payment processing
   - Validate billing dispute procedures

2. **SLA Management**
   - Document service level agreements
   - Set up SLA monitoring
   - Define SLA breach procedures
   - Establish reporting cadence

3. **Incident Management**
   - Review incident classification
   - Test incident logging
   - Verify incident routing
   - Validate resolution tracking

### Phase 3: Beta Testing

1. **Beta Customer Selection**
   - Identify suitable beta customers
   - Obtain customer consent
   - Set expectations
   - Define success criteria

2. **Beta Launch**
   - Onboard beta customers
   - Monitor customer experience
   - Track operational metrics
   - Collect feedback

3. **Beta Evaluation**
   - Analyze operational metrics
   - Review customer feedback
   - Identify issues and improvements
   - Validate readiness for full launch

### Phase 4: Launch Preparation

1. **Launch Plan**
   - Define go-live date
   - Create launch checklist
   - Assign responsibilities
   - Plan communication

2. **Final Validation**
   - Review all operational systems
   - Confirm team readiness
   - Verify support capacity
   - Check monitoring systems

3. **Go-Live Preparation**
   - Brief all teams
   - Activate monitoring
   - Prepare for customer onboarding
   - Establish war room if needed

## Best Practices

### Operational Readiness

**Do**:
- Validate all support processes thoroughly
- Test escalation paths with real scenarios
- Ensure documentation is complete and accessible
- Verify team training and confidence
- Plan for peak capacity
- Establish clear success metrics

**Don't**:
- Skip support process testing
- Assume processes will work
- Launch without capacity planning
- Ignore team readiness concerns
- Rush through validation
- Overlook edge cases

### Beta Testing

**Do**:
- Select representative beta customers
- Set clear expectations
- Monitor closely
- Collect detailed feedback
- Address issues promptly
- Document learnings

**Don't**:
- Skip beta testing
- Use only friendly customers
- Ignore negative feedback
- Rush through beta period
- Launch with unresolved issues
- Forget to thank beta customers

### Launch Validation

**Do**:
- Validate all operational metrics
- Ensure support team is ready
- Confirm monitoring is active
- Have rollback plan ready
- Communicate launch plan clearly
- Celebrate milestones

**Don't**:
- Launch without validation
- Ignore warning signs
- Skip final checks
- Launch without support coverage
- Forget to monitor post-launch
- Overlook team morale

### Support Transition

**Do**:
- Provide comprehensive documentation
- Train support team thoroughly
- Establish clear escalation paths
- Set up regular check-ins
- Monitor support metrics
- Offer ongoing assistance

**Don't**:
- Hand off without training
- Assume support team knows everything
- Skip documentation
- Disappear after launch
- Ignore support feedback
- Forget to celebrate success

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario: Beta Testing Reveals Issues

1. Assess severity of issues
2. Prioritize fixes
3. Work with partner to resolve
4. Extend beta period if needed
5. Retest with beta customers
6. Document resolutions

### Scenario: Support Team Not Ready

1. Identify specific gaps
2. Provide additional training
3. Create support resources
4. Conduct mock scenarios
5. Validate readiness
6. Delay launch if necessary

### Scenario: Operational Metrics Below Target

1. Identify root causes
2. Analyze process bottlenecks
3. Implement improvements
4. Monitor impact
5. Validate metrics improve
6. Document optimizations

### Scenario: Partner Wants to Skip Beta Testing

1. Explain importance of beta testing
2. Share risks of skipping
3. Propose minimal beta approach
4. Document decision and risks
5. Increase post-launch monitoring
6. Plan for rapid response

### Scenario: Launch Date Pressure

1. Assess true readiness
2. Identify critical vs. nice-to-have items
3. Discuss risks with stakeholders
4. Make data-driven recommendation
5. Document decision rationale
6. Plan mitigation strategies

## Troubleshooting

### Partner Not Appearing in Dashboard

- Verify you're assigned as PSM owner
- Check partner's current gate (you only see Gate 3)
- Confirm partner completed Gate 2
- Contact PAM to verify assignment

### Cannot Complete Questionnaire

- Ensure you have PSM role assigned
- Verify all operational validations complete
- Check for required documentation
- Provide digital signature

### Beta Testing Issues

- Review beta customer selection
- Check operational systems
- Verify monitoring is active
- Analyze error logs
- Work with TPM if technical issues
- Extend beta period if needed
